Executive Summary

What this dashboard is, what each view does, and the buying group it was built to serve.

Modern Health Website Redesign
A strategy tool built to guide the MH website redesign.

This dashboard maps a 10-person buying committee to the site architecture, scores every page by strategic priority, and surfaces the content decisions that matter most. The goal is to make sure the right person finds the right answer in as few clicks as possible — and to identify exactly where the current site fails to do that.

The Case for a Holistic Redesign

The existing Modern Health website was built without a documented understanding of who visits it, what they're trying to accomplish, or how they move through it. Without that foundation, every content and navigation decision was made on instinct — what the team felt was worth surfacing, how the product was internally organized, what looked credible. The result is a site built around assumptions rather than audiences.

No persona definition
The site was not built with a clear model of who the buyers are, what roles they hold, or what job each person is trying to do. A 10-person buying committee was navigating a site designed as if one type of visitor existed.
No user story mapping
Without documented user stories, there was no way to know which pages mattered most, which were missing, or where buyers were hitting dead ends. Content investment had no strategic anchor — effort went where it felt right, not where it was needed.
Structure reflects the company, not the buyer
Navigation and content organization mirrored internal team structure and product thinking rather than how buyers evaluate, compare, and build the internal case. The site spoke to itself instead of routing each visitor to their answer.
A page-by-page visual refresh won't fix this. Updating design without changing the underlying logic leaves every assumption intact. A holistic approach — starting from who the audience is, what job they need to do, and what path they take — changes what gets built, where it lives, and how it's written. This dashboard establishes that audience-first foundation, then uses it to make decisions about architecture, content priority, and conversion before a single page is designed.
The Dashboard

Six interconnected views. Each one answers a different strategic question about the redesign.

Heat Map
What it shows
Every page in the sitemap scored by strategic importance across all 10 personas.
Why it exists
Not all pages deserve equal design investment. This view tells you which pages carry the evaluation — where buyers spend time, where deals stall, and where content effort will have the most impact.
Site Architecture
What it shows
The full 73-page IA mapped across all 6 nav sections, with heat scores overlaid on each page.
Why it exists
See how the site is structured, how sections relate to each other, and where the new Solutions section fits. Understand which areas are deep vs. shallow before any page-level design decisions are made.
User Flows
What it shows
Per-persona journey maps — how each buying group member moves through the site to complete their job to be done.
Why it exists
Reveals which pages personas share, where paths diverge, and what conversion action each journey is driving toward. This is the foundation for every cross-link and CTA placement decision in the design.
Homepage Brief
What it shows
A strategic routing brief — what belongs above the fold, which CTAs to show, and how to split the HR buyer vs. broker paths.
Why it exists
The homepage has to route 10 different personas simultaneously without confusing any of them. This brief defines exactly what the homepage needs to do and makes the case for each decision.
Wireframe
What it shows
An interactive prototype — walk through any page in the sitemap as a specific persona following a user story.
Why it exists
Brings the strategy to life. See page purpose, primary audiences, cross-links, and which user stories route through each page. Use it to pressure-test the IA before any design work begins.
Site Navigation Overview

The site is organized into 6 top-level sections. Each serves a different strategic purpose in the buyer journey.

Who We Serve
5 pages
Employers · Members · Consultants · Health Plans · Channel Partners
Audience-specific entry points that route each visitor to content relevant to their role. Each sub-page addresses the specific questions, concerns, and conversion goals of that audience — and signals that Modern Health understands who they are.
Primary audiences: HR / Benefits Manager · Benefits Consultant · Member
Our Approach
5 pages
Adaptive Care · Clinical Quality & Standards · Responsible AI · Partnering with Your Team · Global Capabilities
The how and why behind Modern Health's model. Buyers evaluating clinical credibility, AI governance, implementation support, and global reach all land here. This section answers the "can I trust them?" question before a buyer is ready to request a demo.
Primary audiences: Benefits Consultant · Legal / Compliance · IT / Security · Total Rewards
SolutionsNew
14 pages
Care Modalities · Care for Every Challenge (12 topic areas)
The product depth section — what Modern Health actually covers and how it works. Elevated from Our Approach in v2 to give product detail direct top-level visibility. This is where buyers map coverage to their population and compare Modern Health against their existing benefits stack.
Primary audiences: HR / Benefits Manager · Total Rewards Professional · Benefits Consultant
Outcomes
5 pages
Economic Value / ROI · Clinical Research · Case Studies · Clinical Research Detail · Case Study Detail
The proof section. ROI data, published clinical research, and named enterprise case studies for buyers who need evidence before they can build the internal business case. The single highest-stakes section for converting a skeptical finance leader or procurement lead.
Primary audiences: Finance Leader · HR / Benefits Manager · Benefits Buyer / Procurement
Insights
8 pages
Blog · Events · Past Webinars · Gated Content (+ detail pages)
Thought leadership, event programming, and downloadable resources. Lower strategic priority for the primary buyer evaluation journey but important for brand authority, content marketing, and consultant-facing materials that brokers can share with their clients.
Primary audiences: Benefits Consultant (gated content) · general awareness
Company
5 pages
About Us · Press · Careers · Work With Us · Provider Opportunities
Corporate identity, media coverage, and talent and provider acquisition. Lower strategic priority for the buyer evaluation journey — most visitors here have already made a decision or are not evaluating Modern Health as a vendor. Serves providers and partners more than buyers.
Primary audiences: Provider · Health Plan / Channel Partner · general
The Buying Group

8 personas make up the buying committee for a Modern Health implementation. Each has a different job to be done, a different entry point into the site, and a different definition of success. The site must serve all of them — simultaneously.
